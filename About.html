The Architect

My name is Tristen C. Metsikassus, and one thing I have come to understand about myself is that I have always felt different.

Growing up, I often felt like the odd one out. Even when I was surrounded by people, there was still a part of me that felt separate, like I was standing in the same room but hearing life through a different wall. What once felt like distance became perspective.

I’ve learned that some people are not broken just because they feel different. Sometimes they are simply listening to life at a deeper volume.

One of the greatest things life has taught me is patience. Patience has been the quiet force behind everything I create. Whether I am writing, drawing, or singing, I have learned that honest art cannot be rushed. A tree forced to grow too fast does not become stronger; it becomes easier to break.

I’ve also realized that all of this creating has never been about chasing the world. It has been about honoring my own cadence. I do not create to satisfy trends or keep up with someone else’s timing. Some things are meant to be built slowly if they are going to be built right.

That same patience, feeling, and observation is what I pour into my work as an author. I don’t just want to write books. I want to build worlds that pull people in, challenge what they expect, and leave them thinking long after they close the page.

Sometimes the deepest rivers make the least noise.
